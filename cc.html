Early Detection of Cervical Cancer using Pap Smear Images and Molecular Biomarkers

Python/Machine Learning


Description:
Precision medicine is the future! It involves using genetic information and physiological conditions to tailor diagnosis, treatment plans, and prevention strategies to individual patients.


Question:
Given the labor-intensive and variable nature of annotating microscopic images, how can we improve the accuracy and consistency of histological analysis to better treat cervical cancer?


Solution:
Multiple machine learning algorithms were trained and compared on cervical cancer datasets to identify the most reliable and explainable model. Techniques such as feature selection, model calibration, and cross-validation were used to optimize performance while ensuring interpretability.

  • Random Forest and Gradient Boosting for tabular feature classification
  • Logistic Regression for baseline medical interpretability
  • SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) for feature importance visualization
  • ROC-AUC and F1-score for evaluation metrics


Utilization:

  • Python, Pandas, Scikit-learn, and Matplotlib
  • Data preprocessing and imputation for medical datasets
  • Model calibration and reliability curve analysis
  • Explainable AI techniques (SHAP, feature heatmaps)
  • Cross-validation for robust generalization testing
